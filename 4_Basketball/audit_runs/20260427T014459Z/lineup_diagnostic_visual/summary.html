lineup-mate effect · cross-stat dual-direction structure

20260427T014459Z · n=167 players · partial-correlation controlling for team pace

headline finding

For most stats, the lineup-mate effect on a focal player's year-over-year change has opposite signs depending on where that player sits in the population's baseline rate. Specialists (top-25% by training-period rate) and role-players (bottom-25%) react to the same lineup change in different directions. This is the empirical foundation for Phase 4: a uniform "context layer" would average the two signals to zero. The model needs a player-baseline-conditional interaction term.
