## 4.2.1 Substrate framing

The NBA substrate decomposes per-game player statistic residuals via partial pooling across residue classes defined by **position × years-pro × role-cohort**. The motivating finding, retained from prior work, is that the BLK × Center coupling reproduces across the 2024-25 and 2025-26 regular seasons under both the v6.1 hierarchical model baseline and the within-cohort mean baseline in 11 of 11 cell tests, with PTS × Center directional and AST × deep-vet null both replicating across method and season. In the v2 manuscript we noted that the NBA substrate applied the broader residue-class partial-pooling discipline without invoking the full RMD-SRC pipeline. In this manuscript, NBA is elevated to the full-pipeline list. Steps 0 through 6 plus the F1–F4 falsifier suite plus a comparative arm against traditional partial pooling have been executed under a pre-registration chain locked at commit-SHAs `db0ed9a` (v1.0), `4d0602d` (v1.1), `1bfdb4c` (v1.2), and `f3ede6c` (v1.3), each committed before any cell-level statistic the relevant amendment would test was inspected.

The substrate's analytical scope is the 2019-20 through 2025-26 regular seasons (n = 2,798 qualifying player-seasons in the union across the seven seasons, after applying the locked ≥ 20-game and ≥ 10-MPG qualification gates). Four observables (PTS per-36, REB per-36, AST per-36, BLK per-36) are tracked across the season axis with a five-season training window (2019-20 through 2023-24) and a two-season held-out window (2024-25 + 2025-26). The pre-registration locks the five-regime trajectory classifier of Step 2 at the paper-canonical thresholds (ε_μ = 0.02 on normalized mean-slope, ε_σ² = 0.05 on normalized variance-slope), the F1–F4 thresholds at their paper-canonical values, and the cross-arm comparison's Cohen κ agreement bands at the v1.1 amendment's locked levels. Two pre-registered diagnostic amendments — v1.2 introduces a parallel arm pair using subagent-adjudicated position bucketing, and v1.3 introduces a parallel pipeline using Hartigan-dip-on-residuals to localize a Path-A measurement question — extend the analysis without modifying any v1.0 threshold or disposition.

## 4.2.2 Path A — full pipeline under the locked specification

Path A runs the locked RMD-SRC pipeline on four arms in parallel: a usage-tier role-cohort arm (`usg`, locked at v1.0); a same-season MPG-tier role-cohort arm (`mpg`, locked at v1.1); and two adjudicated-position variants of the same arms (`usg_adj`, `mpg_adj`, locked at v1.2). The four arms share the same qualifying-player set; they differ only in the operationalization of the role-cohort axis and, for the `_adj` variants, in the position bucket assignment for 230 of the 792 union players whose Yahoo eligibility data implies multi-bucket or metadata-disagreeing positional eligibility (Section 4.2.3).

**F1 (substrate validity)** does not fire on any arm × observable combination. The additive linear model on partition variables (position, years-pro bucket, role-cohort, season dummies) explains 8.4% to 24.2% of per-game per-36 variance — far below the 90% threshold. The NBA substrate's variance is structured enough that the framework's machinery is justified to engage; it is not absorbed by simple additive cell effects.

**F2 (decomposability)** does not fire on any arm. Per the v1.0 specification, "terminates cleanly" counts Stationary cells as automatically clean and non-Stationary cells only if they pass Step 3 response validation and produce a successful Step 4 sub-partition. Edge cells (cells with one moment-slope inside the threshold band and one outside) are explicitly excluded from the F2 denominator per v1.0 §3.2. Under the locked spec on Path A, no non-Stationary cell passes Step 3 (see below), so the F2 numerator is carried entirely by the Stationary count. The arm-by-arm fractions are 60.4% (`usg`), 69.4% (`mpg`), 55.9% (`usg_adj`), and 51.7% (`mpg_adj`) — all above the 50% threshold, with the most-resolved arm (`mpg_adj`, K = 25) sitting closest to the firing line.

**F3 (lossy decomposition)** is vacuous on every arm because Step 4's eligible-input set is empty (no Step 3 survivors to sub-partition).

**F4 (regime non-transfer)** fires on all 16 (arm × observable) combinations. The Cohen κ between training-window regime labels and holdout-window regime labels ranges from −0.149 to +0.097, with means of +0.015 (`usg`), −0.042 (`mpg`), +0.022 (`usg_adj`), and −0.024 (`mpg_adj`), all far below the locked 0.40 threshold. The framework's dynamic-classification layer does not transfer across the train→holdout boundary at this substrate's resolution. This is the direct cross-substrate analog of Migration's GEO_F4 firing (paper §4.5).

**Step 5 (cell-signature transfer)** narrowly fails on all four arms. Mean column-wise Pearson r between the training-window cell-signature matrix and the holdout-window matrix is 0.796 (`usg`), 0.722 (`mpg`), 0.737 (`usg_adj`), and 0.693 (`mpg_adj`), each below the locked 0.80 threshold. The column-level decomposition is informative: within-cell standard deviation transfers at r ≈ 0.99 across all four arms, the within-cell role-cohort response coefficient transfers at r = 0.62 to 0.93, while the regime-label column transfers at r = 0.03 to 0.28. **The static decomposition transfers; the dynamic regime layer does not.** This is the same epistemic shape as Migration §4.5.3.

The **comparative arm versus v6.1 LOCKED** returns 0 PASS / 344 TIE / 0 LOSE across all four arms (the denominator is K × 4 observables summed across arms = 19·4 + 21·4 + 21·4 + 25·4 = 344). Because Step 3 flags every non-Stationary cell under Path A, the v1.0 §9.3 PASS criterion (regime_A ∈ non-Stationary AND response-validated AND per-cell transfer r ≥ 0.50) cannot be met by any cell. The LOSE criterion (regime_A non-Stationary AND r < 0.50 AND regime flip) similarly cannot fire because the response-validation gate precedes both. Method A and Method B tie on every cell. The cleanest substantive read is that the framework's added machinery, on this substrate under the locked spec, does not surface a differentiation from traditional partial pooling at the per-cell verdict level.

Step 3 itself produces a substantive substrate-level finding under Path A. The Hartigan dip test on within-cell observable distributions pooled across the training window returns p_dip = 0.000 for every cell × observable on every arm — 0 of 76 cells on `usg`, 0 of 84 on `mpg`, 0 of 84 on `usg_adj`, 0 of 100 on `mpg_adj`. The LOSO 5-fold check returns 66/76 stable on `usg` and similar across the other arms (regime classification is robust to dropping a single training season), and the permutation-stability check returns 20/76 (slope-driven non-Stationary regimes lose their classification under index permutation, as expected). The dip check fails universally because per-game per-36 distributions are intrinsically multimodal at the (player × game) level — within any cell, a starter at 32 MPG and a rotation player at 14 MPG contribute structurally distinct per-game distributions, and the pooled cell-level distribution is bimodal regardless of how the partition's role-cohort axis is operationalized. This pattern mirrors Migration's v1.4 amendment diagnosis exactly (Migration §4.5.2, footnote). Under Path A we report the firing as documented without modification, per the framework's discipline of preserving falsifier firings rather than rescuing them. The substantive consequence is that F4 carries the entire dynamic-layer failure signal on its own.

## 4.2.3 The v1.2 position adjudication

A pre-registered v1.2 amendment introduces a position-bucket adjudication for the 230 players in the qualifying-union whose Yahoo eligible-position string implies multi-bucket eligibility under the v1.0 inclusive Test 1 classifier (196 players) or whose Yahoo single-bucket eligibility disagrees with the metadata position string (34 players). The remaining 562 union players keep their v1.0 bucket assignment.

Adjudication is performed by 230 independent subagents (Sonnet model, one player per agent), each receiving the same locked prompt template (v1.2 §2.3) instantiated with the player's name, metadata position string, v1.0 bucket assignment, Yahoo eligibility, height, debut year, last-seen season, and per-36 career averages for REB, AST, BLK, and FG3M across the 2019-26 window. The locked decision criteria privilege the player's on-court archetype during the analysis window over their metadata or career-aggregate labels; the `no_fit` disposition routes to a fourth `Positionless` position bucket whose substrate behavior is itself a pre-registered subject of inquiry. Each agent returns a structured verdict via a StructuredOutput tool with a locked schema.

The 230 verdicts complete without failure (no schema-invalid outputs, no agent timeouts). The bucket counts are 110 Forward, 60 Center, 60 Guard, and **0 Positionless**. The 0 Positionless verdict is one of the three pre-committed dispositions on the Positionless hypothesis (v1.2 §5) and is reported here as a substantive negative: under deliberate inclusivity scrutiny with player-specific signals, the three-bucket position partition is sufficient for the 2019-26 NBA substrate. The override rate against the v1.0 metadata bucket is 42.6% (98 of 230 verdicts flip the metadata assignment). The flip matrix is dominated by two patterns: 50 Guard → Forward (the metadata systematically under-classifies modern wing forwards as Guards — Nicolas Batum, DeMar DeRozan, Ben Simmons, Jaylen Brown, Josh Hart, Dillon Brooks among them) and 46 Forward → Center (the metadata systematically under-classifies stretch-fives and modern paint-anchors as Forwards — Anthony Davis, Giannis Antetokounmpo, Kevin Love, Mason Plumlee, Porzingis, Olynyk, Powell, Gibson). Two flips go Forward → Guard (Luka Dončić and Tyrese Martin). Zero Center → any-other and zero Guard → Center flips materialize, suggesting the big-versus-guard chasm is correctly preserved by the metadata at the categorical boundary.

The adjudicated partitions produce K = 21 (`usg_adj`) and K = 25 (`mpg_adj`) cells after the locked sparse-cell collapse, versus K = 19 (`usg`) and K = 21 (`mpg`) for the metadata-bucketed arms. The K increase is concentrated in newly-resolved Center cells (Center × {Rookie, Soph-Early, Mid} × {Mid-usage, Low-usage} or × {Rotation, Bench, Starter}), which materialize because the Forward → Center flips push enough volume into Center to clear the 50-player-season-total and 5-per-season floors at multiple years-pro × role-cohort combinations. The cross-arm regime-label Cohen κ matrix (v1.1 §3 expanded to four arms by v1.2 §3) shows usage-tier vs adjudicated-usage-tier on BLK at κ = 0.62 (high-agreement band) — the adjudication's effect on BLK regime classification is small. The same comparison on PTS is κ = 0.57 (partial agreement); on REB κ = 0.34; on AST κ = 0.38. The MPG-tier vs adjudicated-MPG-tier comparison is the most-altered pair, with κ = 0.00 on PTS and κ = 0.05 on AST — the new cells materialized by the adjudication change the MPG-arm regime distribution substantially. Across pairs, the AST observable is the most cross-arm-stable (κ = 0.31 to 0.40 across all six pair × observable combinations).

## 4.2.4 The v1.3 dip-on-residuals diagnostic — substrate-level multimodality

Path A's Step 3 dip-over-fire raises a localization question that v1.3 pre-registers and runs as a diagnostic amendment in the discipline-spirit of Migration's v1.10 ARM-B (paper §4.5.6): does the dip fail because the substrate is genuinely multimodal at the within-cell level, or because the locked Step-3 check is testing on raw values when residuals (against a cell-mean baseline) would be unimodal? The amendment locks before any dip-on-residual p-value is inspected and pre-commits three dispositions on the fraction of cells passing Check D (dip on r_pg = y_pg − μ_cell): ≥ 0.50 localizes the over-fire as measurement-on-raw-values; [0.20, 0.50) is partial localization; < 0.20 confirms substrate-level multimodality.

Under v1.3, Check D returns 0 of 76 on `usg`, 0 of 84 on `mpg`, 0 of 84 on `usg_adj`, and 0 of 100 on `mpg_adj`. **Mean Frac(Check D PASS) across the four arms is 0.0%.** This lands the third pre-committed disposition: substrate-level multimodality is confirmed. The dip-over-fire from Path A is not a measurement artifact. Even after subtracting the cell-mean baseline, within-cell residual distributions remain multimodal at the per-game per-36 level. The locked partition (position × years-pro × role-cohort, in either USG or MPG variant, with or without adjudication) does not fully resolve within-cell role heterogeneity at NBA scale: a starter and a bench player within the same Mid-usage or Rotation cell produce structurally distinct per-game distributions, and the cell mean does not summarize them.

This is the NBA substrate's measurement-level diagnostic localization. It is the discipline-analog of Migration's v1.10 ARM B (rank-3 latent corridor stationarity, §4.5.6) — both substrates have a pre-registered diagnostic that localizes the substrate-level cause of the regime-layer non-transfer signal that F4 reports. In Migration, the diagnostic localizes to a measurement of stationarity at the latent layer; in NBA, the diagnostic localizes to a measurement of within-cell multimodality at the per-game per-36 layer. In both cases the diagnostic amendment preserves the original falsifier firing without modification of the locked threshold (Path A's F4 firings and Path A's Step 3 dip-over-fire are documented as substrate-shape findings, and the v1.3 amendment confirms the substrate-level reading of both).

Path A's downstream falsifier dispositions are unchanged under Path B because Check D's residual_clean disposition propagates the same Step-3 failure pattern: residual_clean = D ∧ B ∧ C = 0/all across every arm, leaving Path B's F2 numerator carried entirely by Stationary count (identical to Path A's), Path B's Step 4 input empty, and Path B's comparative arm at 0 PASS / 344 TIE / 0 LOSE. The methodology paper reports both paths with equal prominence; the Path-B verdict is that Path A's discipline-pure firing was correct in the precise operational sense.

## 4.2.5 What this substrate demonstrates

NBA enters the cross-substrate full-pipeline list at the same depth as Migration. Both substrates execute the locked RMD-SRC pipeline through all six steps plus the F1–F4 falsifier suite plus a pre-registered diagnostic amendment that localizes a substrate-level finding without modifying the original falsifier's threshold. Both substrates exhibit the same epistemic shape: static decomposition transfers (Migration's gravity baseline at McFadden R² 0.63 / 0.74 and latent corridor split-half congruence 0.95; NBA's σ_within at r ≈ 0.99 and β_role-cohort at r 0.62 to 0.93 across windows), while the dynamic regime-classification layer does not (Migration's GEO_F4 at κ −0.06 / +0.02 and NBA's F4 at mean κ ≈ 0). Both substrates' diagnostic amendments produce measurement-level findings that explain why the dynamic layer fails to transfer without re-licensing the framework to claim what its falsifier ruled out.

In NBA the comparative arm against v6.1 LOCKED returns 0 PASS / 344 TIE / 0 LOSE — the framework's added machinery does not differentiate from traditional partial pooling under the locked spec. The cleanest read is that NBA cells, under any of the four locked operationalizations (USG-tier with metadata or adjudicated positions, MPG-tier with metadata or adjudicated positions), are dominated by Stationary trajectories (around 30% to 42% per arm) and Edge trajectories (around 30% to 42% per arm) whose dynamic content sits below the Step-2 slope-threshold band. The non-Stationary cells that do classify are flagged at Step 3 by the substrate's structural within-cell multimodality (confirmed under v1.3). The framework's machinery is not over-reaching; it is correctly reporting that the substrate, at the resolution the partition provides, does not carry the dynamic content that would let RMD-SRC outperform a static pooling baseline.

The v1.2 position adjudication finding is a separately reportable substrate-level contribution. The 0 Positionless verdicts demonstrate that the three-bucket position partition is sufficient under deliberate adjudication scrutiny with player-specific signals — a methodological data point about how positionless modern basketball actually is at the categorical level. The 42.6% override rate against metadata positions surfaces a systematic mis-classification pattern (modern wings are mis-bucketed as Guards; modern paint-anchors are mis-bucketed as Forwards) that propagates to the v1.0 partition's Center cell counts. The cross-arm Cohen κ matrix shows that the adjudication's effect on regime classification is uneven across observables (BLK most-stable at κ ≈ 0.62 cross-arm; PTS least-stable at κ ≈ 0.00 on the mpg-vs-mpg_adj pair), giving the methodology paper a partition-axis sensitivity finding to report alongside the substrate's main dispositions.

The NBA substrate's section in the methodology paper is now the second full-pipeline exemplar after Migration. Its load-bearing contributions are (i) the cross-substrate confirmation that the static-decomposition-transfers / dynamic-regime-classification-fails pattern is not Migration-specific; (ii) the discipline-pure execution of a four-amendment SHA-locked pre-registration chain with all amendments preserved at their lock SHAs and all falsifier firings preserved without modification; (iii) the subagent-adjudicated position bucketing as a substrate-specific methodological contribution that surfaces a metadata mis-classification pattern at scale; and (iv) the v1.3 dip-on-residuals diagnostic as a measurement-level localization of the substrate's within-cell multimodality, paralleling Migration's v1.10 ARM-B latent-stationarity localization in discipline and substantive content.
