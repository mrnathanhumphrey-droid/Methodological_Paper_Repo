## 1. Hypothesis

**H1 (Carnegie 2025's untested durability predictor, formalized):** Bukelization episodes split into two structural sub-types based on the institutional vehicle of consolidation:

| Sub-type | Definition | Examples (in-sample candidates) |
|---|---|---|
| **Party-state fusion** | A political party becomes structurally integrated with the state apparatus; succession can happen within the party | HUN (Fidesz), POL (PiS), VEN (PSUV), maybe BLR (BPRYU/Lukashenko-aligned umbrella) |
| **Personalist-leader** | Consolidation centers on a single individual; succession crisis is existential | SLV (Bukele), TUR (Erdoğan), TUN (Saied), USA (Trump) |

**H2 (durability prediction):** Personalist-leader cases are LESS durable in the consolidation phase — succession crisis can trigger collapse-of-consolidation. Party-state cases are MORE durable — institutional inertia persists past leader departure.

**H3 (reversal/recovery prediction):** Personalist cases recover FASTER once the leader exits (e.g., dies, term-limits out, suffers election defeat). Party-state cases recover SLOWER because party-controlled institutions resist reform even after coalition turnover.

### Prediction B — Cross-case speed test

Personalist-leader cases should show:
- **Faster initial collapse** (single dramatic event triggers consolidation)
- **More fragile mid-game** (succession concerns visible in V-Dem sub-indicators)
- **Faster recovery on leader exit** (institutional capture is shallower)

Party-state cases should show:
- **Slower initial collapse** (incremental legislative capture)
- **Robust mid-game** (institutional inertia)
- **Slower recovery even with election turnover** (party-controlled institutions resist)

**Quantitative test:** Predict that the average libdem collapse rate (Δ/yr) is HIGHER for personalist cases than party-state cases.

## 8. Results — first fit (fired 2026-05-25)

### H2 (speed-by-classification) — NULL

Mann-Whitney U test (alternative: personalist rates more negative than party-state):
- Personalist (n=7): mean rate −0.0297/yr, median −0.0343/yr
- Party-state (n=5): mean rate −0.0269/yr, median −0.0326/yr
- U=16.0, p=0.4381 → NOT significant

**H2 NOT SUPPORTED on first fit.** Speed of consolidation does NOT differ significantly by classification.

**Walkback**: H2 (collapse speed prediction) walked back. The mechanism — single-event-driven vs incremental-legalistic — is still real, but it doesn't cleanly cleave along the party-state vs personalist axis. SLV (personalist) is fastest, but VEN (party-state, Chávez-era was personalist-flavored) is 2nd fastest. The single-event-vs-incremental distinction is orthogonal to party-state-vs-personalist.

### H1 + H3 (durability + recovery) — STILL OPEN

These remain testable only in the forward 2026-2030 window. Key in-sample confirmations from the existing data:
- **VEN (party-state, PSUV) survived Chávez's 2013 death + continued backsliding under Maduro** → party-state durability supported

- **POL recovery 2023-2025 supports party-state stalling**: even with Tusk PM win, +0.228 libdem recovery is happening alongside Constitutional Tribunal still PiS-controlled

These are all consistent with H1/H3 but not formal tests. Real test = forward observation.
