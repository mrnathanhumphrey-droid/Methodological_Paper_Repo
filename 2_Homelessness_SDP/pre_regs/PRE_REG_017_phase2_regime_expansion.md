## 1. Hypothesis

**H1 (regime stability under expansion):** Applying PRE_REG_003's H1 classification rules to ~25 additional candidate countries, the 6-regime typology absorbs them without requiring a new regime AND boundary cases concentrate in physically intermediate countries (e.g., countries with multiple competing hazard channels). No new regime emerges.

**H2 (geographic-mechanism predictions):**
- Tropical cyclone belts → Regime 3 (storm-dominant)
- Major subduction-zone / collision-zone countries with limited cyclone exposure → Regime 6 (EQ-dominant)
- Large river-basin countries with sustained monsoon → Regime 2 (steady-high-flood) candidates
- Continental countries with mixed hazards → Regime 4 (mixed)

## 3. Falsifiers

- **F1**: ≥3 of 7 Caribbean candidates fit NONE of existing regimes → typology incomplete for Caribbean
- **F2**: ≥3 of 7 South Pacific candidates classified non-Regime-3 (other than data-sparse) → cyclone-belt mechanism fails for Pacific
- **F3**: 0 of 4 Regime 6 candidates confirmed → Regime 6 is sparse (HTI/NPL/TUR/CHL/ECU/ITA only; no expansion)
- **F4**: A new regime needs to be added (i.e., a country fits no existing regime cleanly) → typology incomplete
- **F5**: ≥2 countries surface as candidates for the (currently sparse) Regime 1 (bimodal-mega-flood) → PAK is not structurally unique

Any 2 of {F1, F2, F4} firing = H1 walked back.

## 4. Methodology

- GIDD Disasters 2008-2024 hazard-type breakdown per candidate country
- Apply PRE_REG_003 H1 rules directly (storm >70% → Regime 3; flood max/median > 30× → Regime 1; EQ >60% → Regime 6; etc.)
- Sufficient-evidence threshold: ≥100K cumulative disaster-IDP for valid classification; lower-count countries marked "data-sparse"
