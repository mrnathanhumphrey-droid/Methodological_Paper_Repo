## 1. USA — Trump II (2025-) [PRIORITY]

### Pre-Trump-II V-Dem trajectory
Per reporting on V-Dem 2025/2026 reports:
- 2024 V-Dem Liberal Democracy Index (LDI): **0.75**
- 2025 V-Dem LDI: **0.57** (lowest score since **1965**)
- One-year drop: **24%** decline in LDI
- World rank: **20th -> 51st** out of 179 nations in a single year
- Magnitude context (V-Dem-quoted): of 25,464 country-year observations 1789–2025, **only 36 (0.14%)** have a larger one-year LDI drop than USA 2024->2025

### Trump II institutional pressure events (chronological, 2025)
All dated events sourced to news outlets cited below.

- **2025-01-24** — Mass firing of **17 federal Inspectors General** ("Friday night purge"). Notification via email citing "changing priorities." District Judge Ana C. Reyes (Sept 2025) ruled the firings violated the IG Act's 30-day congressional notice + substantive rationale requirement; declined reinstatement. (Sources: Wikipedia "2025 dismissals of U.S. inspectors general"; NPR 2025-01-25; Lawfare; Government Executive 2025-09)
- **2025-03-07** — Cancellation of **$400M federal funding** to Columbia University.
- **2025-03** (approx) — **$8.7B federal grants + $256M contracts** at Harvard placed under review.
- **2025-04** — Harvard sues federal government over **$2.7B funding freeze**.
- **2025-05-11** — Levitsky, Way & Ziblatt NYT op-ed asserts US has crossed into competitive authoritarianism (referenced in Fair Observer summary).
- **2025-07** — Columbia agrees to pay **$221M settlement** to Trump administration to unfreeze funding.
- **2025-09** — Federal judge rules Harvard funding terminations were a "targeted, ideologically-motivated assault" and unlawful.
- **2025-09-30** — Trump officials take step to end Harvard's federal funding eligibility.
- **2025 (course of year)** — DOJ ordered to investigate **ActBlue** and **Open Society Foundation**; IRS reportedly directed to target Democratic donors (per Levitsky/Way/Ziblatt). FCC opened investigations into **ABC, CBS, PBS, NPR, Comcast**. Lawsuits filed by Trump against **Wall Street Journal, New York Times**.

### Key papers / reports
- **V-Dem Institute, Democracy Report 2025** ("25 Years of Autocratization – Democracy Trumped?") — Nord et al., SSRN abstract 5227625; PDF at v-dem.net/documents/60/. Tags USA as "the fastest evolving episode of autocratization the USA has been through in modern history" and as potentially "the fastest autocratizing country in contemporary history that does not involve a coup d'état."
- **V-Dem Institute, Democracy Report 2026** ("Unraveling the Democratic Era?", 10th edition) — v-dem.net/documents/75/. Records the LDI drop 0.75→0.57 and identifies the specific sub-indicators driving it.
- **Carrier, McKenzie & Carothers, Thomas (Aug 2025)** — *U.S. Democratic Backsliding in Comparative Perspective*, Carnegie Endowment. Compares Trump II to **Brazil, Ecuador, El Salvador, Hungary, India, Poland, Türkiye** across three dimensions: **Focus, Rapidity, Severity**. Distinctive Trump II features per the report: (i) intra-executive dominance focus, (ii) **delegitimization** rather than institutionalized capture of horizontal checks, (iii) coercive use of the federal funding ecosystem to pressure civil society. "Uncommon early momentum… startling speed and multilayered nature stands in contrast to the incremental, piece-by-piece nature of backsliding in most other troubled democracies."
- **Levitsky, S. & Way, L. (2025)** — *The Path to American Authoritarianism: What Comes After Democratic Breakdown*, Foreign Affairs. PDF mirrored at ash.harvard.edu. Argues USA on path to competitive authoritarianism in Trump's second term.
- **Levitsky, Way & Ziblatt (2025)** — NYT op-ed May 11 + follow-up Foreign Affairs piece "The Price of American Authoritarianism" (Dec 2025). Asserts US has *already* crossed into competitive authoritarian rule. Reversibility is explicit thesis.
- **Tandfonline (Nord et al. 2025)** — "State of the world 2024: 25 years of autocratization – democracy trumped?" Democratization 32(4). DOI 10.1080/13510347.2025.2487825.
- **Tandfonline (2026)** — "State of the world 2025: unraveling the democratic era?" Democratization. DOI 10.1080/13510347.2026.2661683.

### V-Dem sub-indicators with largest 2024->2025 declines (USA)
Per V-Dem-quoted reporting:
- Executive oversight by bodies other than the legislature
- Legislature investigating unconstitutional/illegal/unethical executive activity in practice
- Government censorship effort over the media
- Freedom of academic and cultural expression
- Rule of law indicators
- Individual liberties

These map to **horizontal accountability** and **diagonal accountability** — V-Dem's framing — being "attacked the most during the first two months of the Trump administration."

### What the literature says about USA's classification
- **V-Dem 2025/2026**: actively autocratizing, fastest-evolving episode in US history, could become "fastest autocratizing country in contemporary history that does not involve a coup."
- **Carnegie (Carrier-Carothers Aug 2025)**: yes, backsliding, but US institutions "remain more resilient than many global counterparts" — speed and scope are the concerns, not yet floor.
- **Levitsky/Way/Ziblatt**: has crossed the line into competitive authoritarianism (May 2025 NYT, Dec 2025 Foreign Affairs).

### Comparison to our 7-case corpus (SLV/HUN/TUR/VEN/POL/TUN/BLR)
Carnegie 2025 explicitly compares Trump II to HUN, POL, TUR, BRA, IND, ECU, SLV. The distinguishing features it identifies — **intra-executive consolidation + delegitimization rather than institutional capture + funding coercion** — most resemble:
- **Bukele/SLV pattern**: rapid, broad-front, uses executive/funding levers; speed-of-takeover comparison is closest match. V-Dem's "fastest autocratization without a coup" framing maps directly onto our pattern 013 ("Bukelization without coup").
- **NOT HUN/POL pattern**: Carnegie explicitly notes Trump II is *not* the incremental, piece-by-piece capture seen in Orbán/PiS. So if our 7-case panel uses HUN as the canonical slow-grind case, USA is the *opposite-extreme* match on the speed axis.

Trajectory shape: USA 2024->2025 single-year LDI drop (≈ −0.18) is in the magnitude range of SLV under Bukele's first state-of-exception year — *not* the HUN 2010→2018 slow slide.

### Open empirical questions
- Does V-Dem 2026 score continue the drop or stabilize at 0.57? (V-Dem v.16 release statement may have full data)
- Carnegie's third dimension "Severity" — where does USA land vs. SLV/HUN/TUR on the floor metric, not just rate?
- Does Levitsky/Way reversibility thesis hold empirically once libdem has dropped 24% in one year? (No prior comparable case has reversed from such a deep one-year drop in a wealthy consolidated democracy.)

## 2. SRB — Aleksandar Vučić (2012-)

### Consolidation chronology
- 2012: Vučić becomes Deputy PM (SNS in power)
- 2014: PM
- 2017: President
- 2022: Re-elected President
- **2014 onward**: V-Dem classifies Serbia as **electoral autocracy**.

### V-Dem LDI trajectory
- 2011: **0.51**
- 2023: **0.25**
- Globally: **5th-largest drop** in LDI; ranked **41st of 45 European countries**

### 2024-2025 crisis chronology
- **2024-11-01**: Novi Sad railway station canopy collapse, **14 dead** (15th and 16th died later from injuries). Canopy was part of a 2021-2024 renovation backed by China's Belt and Road. Vucic initially claimed the canopy "had not been part of the recent renovations."
- **2024-11-04**: Transport Minister Goran Vesić resigns.
- **2024-11-05**: First large Novi Sad protest. Tens of thousands. Smashed windows at City Hall.
- **2024-11-21**: 11 arrested in connection with collapse, including Vesić (released Nov 27).
- **2024-12-30**: 13 formally indicted, including Vesić.
- **2025-01-28**: PM Miloš Vučević resigns after ruling-party associates of his son assaulted a female student in Novi Sad, breaking her jaw.
- **Nov 2024 – ongoing**: nationwide university blockade. Over **3,500 protests/assemblies** recorded March-April 2025. Termed largest student-led mobilization in Europe since 1968.
- **2026-05-22/23**: Latest mass rally in Belgrade; police clashes, tear gas, students demanding early elections.

### Key papers / reports
- **Milačić, F. (2024)** — "Democratic Backsliding Through Legislative Capture in Serbia: A One-Man Show," *ANNALS of the American Academy of Political and Social Science*, DOI 10.1177/00027162251316346.
- **BTI 2025/2026 Serbia country reports** — bti-project.org. Blog post "One man, One state: Vučić and Serbia's Student Protests" (Aug 2025).
- **The Lancet Regional Health – Europe** (2025) — "Serbia is in crisis" (public-health/governance crossover piece).
- **European Western Balkans** ongoing coverage.

### EU dimension
EU funding at stake reportedly ~**€1.5B ($1.8B)** linked to backsliding. EU accession process has *not* constrained Vučić in the way it constrained PiS-era Poland (where exit from PiS came via 2023 election, partly under EU funds-freeze pressure). Vučić balances EU + Russia + China (Belt-and-Road infrastructure including the Novi Sad station itself).

### Comparison to 7-case corpus
Closest match: **HUN (Orbán)** — long-running incumbent (Vučić 13 years like Orbán 14 years by 2024), electoral-autocracy classification, EU member-state-adjacent leverage points. Differs in that SRB is *aspirant* EU not member, so EU-funds lever is weaker than HUN/POL.

## 3. BGD — Sheikh Hasina (2009-2024) [REVERSED]

### Consolidation chronology
- 2009: Hasina/Awami League return to power.
- 2009-2024: 15 years. Per V-Dem and Freedom House: harassment of political opponents, co-option of state institutions.
- **2024-01**: Parliamentary election. BNP and most opposition boycotted. Hasina/AL win nearly all seats. Widely viewed as illegitimate.
- **2024-07**: Supreme Court reinstates government job quotas (30% to freedom-fighter descendants). Student "quota reform" protests begin.
- **2024-07/08**: Hasina calls protesters "grandchildren of Razakars" (collaborator slur). Protests intensify. ≥**800 protesters killed** within a week (per Australian Institute of International Affairs / EUAA / al-Jazeera reporting).
- **2024-08-05**: Hasina **flees country**.
- **2024-08-08**: Interim government installed under **Muhammad Yunus** (Nobel laureate). Parliament dissolved.
- **2026 (planned)**: 13th National Parliamentary Election. IRI + ANFREL pre-election assessments published.

### V-Dem LDI trajectory
- 2023: **0.096**
- 2024: **0.068** — *minimum value since at least 1971*
- 2025 (per V-Dem 2026 report): Bangladesh **descended into the closed autocracy category in 2025** per V-Dem 2026

### The reversal signal in V-Dem
**Important**: V-Dem 2026 (data through 2025) shows Bangladesh's *classification* **worsening** to **closed autocracy** despite the Yunus interim government taking power Aug 2024. The institutional damage from 2009-2024 AL rule + the legitimacy-vacuum / interim-government status post-Aug-2024 + ongoing violence/political-retribution problems noted by Freedom House have not yet produced a libdem rebound in the 2025 data.

This is **the key empirical finding for our pattern 013**: a successful protest-driven ouster does *not* automatically register as democratic restoration in V-Dem within the first calendar year. The signal we'd expect — a libdem rebound — has not yet appeared in V-Dem 2026.

### Key papers / reports
- **Freedom House, Freedom in the World 2025: Bangladesh** — documents the 2024 ouster and first-five-months interim assessment.
- **House of Commons Library, "Bangladesh: The fall of the Hasina Government and recent political developments"** (CBP-10096).
- **Wikipedia "July Revolution (Bangladesh)"** — describes as "the world's first successful Gen Z revolution," led by Students Against Discrimination.
- **Journal of Democracy** — "Will Bangladesh's Massive Democratic Experiment Work?" (online exclusive 2025/2026).
- **The Diplomat** Feb 2026 — "Bangladesh's 2026 Election Is a Litmus Test for Global Democratic Revival."
- **EUAA situation update** (one-year mark).

### Comparison to other youth-protest-driven reversals
Closest precedent matches:
- **TUN 2011** (Arab Spring) — also student/youth-led, also ousted long-incumbent. TUN's libdem rebound in V-Dem registered fast then eroded post-2021. BGD's first-year V-Dem signal (closed autocracy classification persisting) is *worse* than TUN's 2011 signal.
- **SRI 2022** (Aragalaya) — youth-led, ousted Rajapaksa. Provides a closer regional analog.
- **NIC 2018** (failed) — counterfactual: youth-led protests crushed; Ortega consolidated further. NIC is the **opposite outcome** to BGD.

### Comparison to 7-case corpus
**BGD is currently the only "reversed" case relevant to our 7-case panel** — TUN reversed-then-re-eroded (Saied 2021). BGD is the cleanest *recent* reversal-via-youth-protest case. Adding BGD to the corpus would specifically test whether libdem-floor cases can rebound.

### Open empirical questions
- Will V-Dem 2027 (2026 data) register the Yunus reforms as a libdem uptick, or does the closed-autocracy classification persist?
- Does the 2026 parliamentary election restore competitive multi-party democracy or replicate the 2024 sham?
- Comparison to TUN post-2011 5-year window: does BGD trace the same arc?

## 4. NIC — Daniel Ortega (2007-) [COMPLETED CONSOLIDATION]

### Consolidation chronology
- 2007: Ortega/FSLN return to power.
- 2008-2013: Competitive authoritarian regime (per McConnell 2024).
- 2014-2017: Consolidation of one-party dominant system.
- **2018-04-18**: Social-security-reform protests erupt. Crackdown.
- **2018 (course of year)**: ≥**325 deaths**, >2,000 injured, hundreds detained/tortured, **52,000+ exiled**. Per HRW + State Dept 2018 country report: torture including electric shocks, beatings, fingernail removal, asphyxiation, rape.
- 2018-2024: V-Dem classifies as **repressive police state**.
- **2021**: Pre-election arrest/disqualification of virtually all opposition candidates. Ortega wins >75%. 7 opposition candidates jailed (Chamorro 13y, Maradiaga 13y, Cruz 9y).
- **2022-02 onward**: Catholic Church property seizures begin. **≥39 Catholic properties** seized over 3.5 years. **1,342 associations/foundations/ministries** dismantled. **>16,500 religious processions banned**. **1,010 attacks** on Catholic Church.
- **2024-11**: Constitutional reform passed (first vote; needs second vote ~2025). Designates Ortega **AND** Murillo as **co-presidents**. ~140 of 200 articles changed; 37 cancelled. Article 14: religious organizations must keep free from "all foreign control" — targets Vatican relationship.

### V-Dem LDI trajectory
- V-Dem 2025 lists Nicaragua among **seven currently regressing** countries.
- Per IDP pattern 013 corpus data: NIC libdem **~0.02** in latest data — at the **floor**.
- Returned to pre-democratic-transition levels.

### Key papers / reports
- **McConnell, Shelley A. (2024)** — "Elite Collusion and Creeping Authoritarianism in Nicaragua: Lessons on Democratic Backsliding from an Outlier Case," *ANNALS of the American Academy of Political and Social Science*, DOI 10.1177/00027162251320222.
- **Ramachandran, S. (Jan 2024)** — "Desk study on aid and democracy: Nicaragua," UNU-WIDER.
- **HRW (2019)** — "Crackdown in Nicaragua: Torture, Ill-Treatment, and Prosecutions of Protesters and Opponents."
- **Tom Lantos Human Rights Commission** hearing on political prisoners.
- **BTI 2026 Nicaragua Country Report** — bti-project.org/en/reports/country-report/NIC.
- **Confidencial.digital** — investigative reporting on Church property seizures.
- **ConstitutionNet** — "A New Absolutist Constitution Tailor-Made for an Authoritarian Couple."

### Why NIC went to floor while POL/HUN/TUR plateaued
Hypotheses surfaced in the literature:
1. **No EU constraint** — POL/HUN constrained by EU funds-freeze, infringement procedures; NIC has none.
2. **2018 mass-casualty inflection** — Ortega crossed a violence threshold (325 deaths) POL/HUN/TUR did not, removing reputational ceiling.
3. **Family-dynasty structure** — Ortega-Murillo co-presidency 2024 has no analog in HUN/POL/TUR; suggests Cuba/Venezuela-family-rule trajectory closer than European backsliders.
4. **Catholic Church as last horizontal-power threat removed 2022-24** — leaves no domestic institutional counterweight.

### Comparison to 7-case corpus
**NIC = endpoint case**. Closest analogs in our panel: **VEN (Maduro)** and **BLR (Lukashenko)** — completed authoritarian consolidation with no functioning electoral competition, floor-level libdem. NIC fits this cluster, not the HUN/POL/TUR "competitive authoritarian" cluster.

### Open empirical questions
- Does Murillo succession (she is co-president by design) hold post-Ortega?
- Are remaining civil-society networks (exiled diaspora, Church-in-exile) viable restoration vectors?

## 5. BRA — Bolsonaro (2019-2022) [FAILED BACKSLIDING]

### Bolsonaro institutional pressure attempts
- 2019-2022: Attacks on Supreme Federal Court (STF), election denial rhetoric, military meetings, disinformation networks.
- 2022-10: Loses election to Lula.
- **2023-01-08**: Supporters storm presidential palace, Supreme Court, Congress in Brasília. One week after Lula inauguration. Bolsonaro never conceded.
- **2025-11-25**: STF convicts Bolsonaro + 7 co-defendants for participating in a **coup plot** to subvert 2022 election results (per CNN reporting).

### What blocked the backsliding (per the literature)
- **Supreme Federal Court (STF)**: explicit protagonism. Operated "outside normal boundaries" to defend democracy. STF justice Alexandre de Moraes central.
- **Election institutions** held.
- **Military refused to act** on Bolsonaro's behalf.
- **Coup-plot defendants prosecuted and convicted** — institutional accountability follow-through.

### V-Dem trajectory + recovery
- V-Dem 2026 explicitly classifies Brazil as a case of **"U-turn democratization"** — autocratization followed by reversal.
- Recent ranking: Brazil **0.7 LDI, rank 28th**, vs USA **0.57, rank 51st** — Brazil now **23 positions ahead of the USA**.
- Lula administration assessed as having "largely demonstrated commitment to upholding democratic norms" with caveats (e.g., 2023 attempted "fake news" Bill 2630, which failed).

### Key papers / reports
- **Carnegie Endowment (April 2025)** — "Democratic Recovery After Significant Backsliding: Emergent Lessons," Carothers et al. Brazil featured.
- **iConnect Blog (2026)** — "Brazil in the V-Dem Democracy Report 2026: Reversal, Resilience, and the Limits of Democratic Recovery."
- **Review of Democracy / CEU (2025-05-23)** — "Brazil's Democratic Resilience: How Institutions Withstood Bolsonaro's Assault."
- **European Parliament Think Tank** — "Brazilian democracy in the aftermath of 8 January" (EPRS_BRI(2023)739354).
- **CNN (2025-11-25)** — "Bolsonaro tested Brazil's democracy. Its Supreme Court stepped in."

### Why BRA is the canonical "failed backsliding" case
1. Aggressor (Bolsonaro) lost his re-election outright — electoral defeat, not negotiated exit.
2. Post-defeat insurrectionary moment (Jan 8) was contained within days.
3. Subsequent prosecution all the way to conviction of the principal — full institutional follow-through, not amnesty.
4. V-Dem registered a **U-turn** in libdem direction post-2022 — unusual; few backsliding cases reverse on V-Dem in this window.

### Comparison to 7-case corpus
**BRA = the control case**. Our 7-case panel (SLV/HUN/TUR/VEN/POL/TUN/BLR) selects on "backsliding happened." Adding BRA as an "attempted-but-failed" case would let us identify *which institutional configurations* (independent constitutional court + apolitical military + losable elections) block the trajectory. POL 2023 also reversed (PiS lost), but via election cycle without the insurrectionary stress-test BRA passed.

### Open empirical questions
- Does Bolsonaro's 2025 conviction affect 2026 election dynamics? Eligibility?
- Is Bolsonaro-family-successor (Eduardo, Carlos) running an analog of the Murillo-co-president succession?
- Does the STF's protagonism create its own institutional-balance risks longer-term?
