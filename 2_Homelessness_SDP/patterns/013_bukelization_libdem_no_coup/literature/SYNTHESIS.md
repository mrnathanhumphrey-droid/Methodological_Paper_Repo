## NARROWED by literature (specific findings to engage with)

### Sub-indicator sequencing — must be corrected
Our deep extraction reported MAGNITUDE rankings (elections-free-fair drops most, then media, then judicial). **The literature's chronological finding is different**:

- **Case-empirics agent**: across SLV/HUN/POL/TUR/VEN/BLR, **Constitutional Court capture is consistently FIRST** chronologically. The captured court then enables electoral law changes, media ownership reforms, civil society restrictions.
- **Sato et al. 2022**: empirically tests horizontal → diagonal → vertical sequencing across 69 episodes (NOT same as our framing)

**Methodological lesson**: V-Dem sub-indicator deltas measure CONSEQUENCES of capture; the chronological ORDER requires event-history (legislative dates, court-packing events). Our magnitude data is **consistent with court-first chronology** (courts capture quickly, then enable bigger downstream changes in elections + media). Writeup must distinguish.

### TUN doesn't fit
- Cordall et al. (2023) call Tunisia "breakdown by disengagement" not "executive aggrandizement"
- Our V-Dem trajectory FITS Bukelization shape, but the mechanism is different (Saied stepped into a vacuum left by Ennahda-Nidaa Tunis collapse)
- **Refinement**: TUN is on the libdem-trajectory shape but mechanistically distinct. Keep it in the corpus as "shape-fit but mechanism-divergent" — useful for showing the shape can emerge from different mechanisms.

### BLR is treated as a negative control
- Modern literature considers BLR already authoritarian by 2001
- Our libdem trajectory shows consolidation 1992-2004, but contemporary "autocratization" frameworks don't classify it as backsliding (no democratic baseline)
- **Refinement**: BLR is a "completed-consolidation case" not an "ongoing backsliding case." Useful boundary marker but not a peer of POL/HUN/SLV/TUR.

### Stalled-recovery configuration (Carnegie 2025)
- Of 25 backsliding episodes since 1990, only 4 have FULLY recovered; only Sri Lanka >5 years sustained (and even SRI reversed in 2019)
- **The textbook STALLED configuration**: captured Constitutional Court survives parliamentary turnover + cohabitation with old-regime presidency
- **POL is the live test of this**: Tusk PM coalition but **Karol Nawrocki (PiS-backed) won June 2025 presidential election** — opposition president + still-captured Constitutional Tribunal
- Our PATTERN_022 (BRA recovery) framing needs to add: POL recovery may be STALLED, not durable. The +0.228 libdem in 2 years is real but the institutional configuration suggests reversal risk.

### Mass emigration is a feedback loop (Auer & Schaub 2024)
- Liberal-leaning citizens self-select out → home electorate becomes more illiberal → accelerates backsliding
- HUN ~360K diaspora, 80% under 40, 33% degree-holders (vs 18% baseline)
- VEN ~7.9M, BLR 200-500K post-2020, TUR 6,000+ academics
- **This is a mechanism we missed**: emigration isn't just a CONSEQUENCE of Bukelization; it's part of the FEEDBACK LOOP that perpetuates it
- **Open quantitative test we can run**: does emigration rate correlate with libdem decline rate across our 7-case corpus?

### Economic ≠ uniform collapse
- VEN/TUR are canonical collapse cases
- POL is the canonical "stable economy under backsliding" (EU single-market floor)
- HUN is "stable stagnation"
- BLR is Russian protectorate (70% trade share)
- SLV debt 95% GDP, IMF-conditioned Bitcoin pullback
- **No simple economic prediction** — Bukelization doesn't imply economic collapse, and economic collapse doesn't imply Bukelization

## STILL OPEN (where our work adds value)

### 1. Party-state fusion vs personalist-leader axis as durability predictor
- Carnegie 2025: this is "the most promising UNTESTED durability predictor"
- Stratifies our 7 cases cleanly: HUN (Fidesz party-state), POL (PiS party-system) vs SLV (Bukele personalist), TUR (Erdoğan personalist), VEN (Chávez/Maduro PSUV partial), BLR (Lukashenko personalist), TUN (Saied personalist)
- **Hypothesis to test**: personalist-leader cases are MORE durable because succession crisis can reverse; party-state cases are LESS reversible because the party structure persists past leader departure
- BUT POL (party-state) is recovering and BLR (personalist) isn't — so the prediction direction isn't obvious

### 2. Reverse brain drain as recovery accelerator
- Poland's 2023+ returning migrants is the under-studied symmetric case
- No quantitative test exists yet
- We can build one: track UN migration data for POL/BRA pre-and-post recovery
- **This is a publishable empirical contribution**

### 3. Speed variation (our finding remains novel)
- Our −0.061/yr (SLV) to −0.014/yr (IND) range
- Sato et al. 2022 didn't decompose speed variation
- Single-event triggers (SLV state-of-exception, TUN self-coup) vs incremental legalistic (HUN, POL, BLR) is a distinction the literature doesn't formalize

### 4. Coordinated resistance within one electoral cycle (the leverage point)
- V-Dem WP #147 says this is the single biggest predictor of fast U-turn
- We can operationalize and test this against our 7-case corpus
- Concrete writing target: a measurable indicator of "coordinated resistance" (joint opposition list / unified protest mobilization / cross-party legislative coalition)

### 5. Slow-burn variant (IND)
- IND fits at 18-24y windows, not 10y
- Federal counter-pressure hypothesis is testable
- Brazil's 2003-2010 partial-backsliding is another slow-burn comparator
- **Slow-burn timescale variation is methodologically distinct from speed variation within fast-burn cases**

### 6. The capture-order question (refined)
- Court-first chronology now established by literature
- Open question: does the SUB-ORDER within court → elections → media → civil society vary by case?
- Worth running: time-stamp the major capture events for each of 7 cases and test ordering

Don't use "Bukelization." Use "**third wave autocratization**" or "**executive-aggrandizement backsliding**." Reserve SLV as a specific case-example.

### Contribution claims (refined)

2. **Speed-variation analysis** (NOVEL): −0.061/yr to −0.014/yr range; distinguishes event-driven vs incremental consolidation
