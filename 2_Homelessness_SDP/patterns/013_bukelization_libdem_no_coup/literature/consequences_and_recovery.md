## 1. Migration / Displacement consequences

### Key papers

- **Auer, D. & Schaub, M. (2024). "Mass Emigration and the Erosion of Liberal Democracy." *International Studies Quarterly* 68(2), sqae026.** — Central thesis: CEE migrants systematically hold more liberal values than non-migrants; their exit is causally associated with deterioration of liberal democracy at home. CEE has lost ~9% of its population to Western Europe since 2004. Frames mass emigration as a *contributing cause* (selection on values) — relevant for our recovery analysis because it implies that backsliding cases have a thinned-out pro-democracy electorate when recovery becomes possible. https://academic.oup.com/isq/article/68/2/sqae026/7641120
- **Gest, J. (2025). *Democratic Drain: Global Migration and the Struggle for Democracy.* Cambridge University Press.** — In-depth interviews + cross-country data across 149 countries; argues global migration filters liberal-leaning citizens out of authoritarian spaces, enabling further backsliding. https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/democratic-drain/D8497D6A38FCA61ADE68CC62235449B9
- **NBER WP 34432 — "Voting with Their Feet: Democratic Backsliding and Market-Minded Migration" (2025, CEPR DP20954).** — Two natural experiments (Israel judicial overhaul; European "Syrian shock"). Difference-in-differences finds causal effect of democratic erosion on out-migration; mass exit emerges *before* formal institutional change is complete. Brain drain creates conditions for populism (a self-reinforcing loop). https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w34432/w34432.pdf
- **CEPR VoxEU column on Auer/Schaub/Gest synthesis: "Migration and regime change: Outflows follow democratic decline, inflows fuel illiberal drift."** https://cepr.org/voxeu/columns/migration-and-regime-change-outflows-follow-democratic-decline-inflows-fuel-illiberal

### Empirical findings on the 7 cases (+ recovery cases)

- **VEN.** Diaspora ~7.9M as of early 2026 (one of the world's largest). Wave 1: ~2M professionals fled during Chávez's 13 years (1999–2013). Wave 2: middle-class then mass exit under Maduro from 2014 onward, driven by political repression (2014 protests), hyperinflation, food/medical shortages, collapse of oil sector. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venezuelan_refugee_crisis ; https://www.csis.org/analysis/persistence-venezuelan-migrant-and-refugee-crisis
- **TUR.** Post-2016 coup attempt: >6,000 academics expelled; >15,000 institutions shut; >130,000 civil servants suspended; >80,000 arrested. "Academics for Peace" petition signers specifically targeted. Sustained intellectual exodus extending through 2023 elections (FP, Just Security). https://www.merip.org/2018/12/turkeys-purge-of-critical-academia/ ; https://www.justsecurity.org/88694/an-exodus-of-professionals-the-end-of-politics-in-turkey/ ; https://foreignpolicy.com/2023/01/27/turkey-elections-erdogan-exodus-population-migration/
- **HUN.** ~360,000 emigrants over Orbán's 14+ years; 41,294 in 2024 alone (record). 80% of emigrants under 40; 33% have university degrees vs 18% of resident population — sharp positive selection. ~18,000 students study abroad annually (population <10M). Government's pronatalist demographic policy has failed to offset. https://fuf.se/en/magasin/korrar/unga-och-hogutbildade-lamnar-ungern-till-foljd-av-orbans-politik/ ; https://www.rferl.org/a/hungary-brain-drain-politics-jobs/32753641.html ; https://vsquare.org/inside-viktor-orbans-failure-to-achieve-his-demographic-goal/
- **POL.** Substantial emigration 2004–2020 (4%→9% of Poles living in another EU MS). Largely *pre-PiS* trajectory (EU accession effect). 2019 PiS tax cut for <26-year-olds was an emigration-stanching policy. **Reverse brain drain documented from 2023 onward** as Tusk takes office and Warsaw tech sector booms. https://www.cgdev.org/article/polands-emigration-rate-falling-people-are-still-worried-about-brain-drain-bogeyman-forbes ; https://www.euronews.com/next/2023/02/03/polands-reverse-brain-drain-meet-the-poles-returning-home-to-work-in-its-booming-tech-sect — *Note for our purposes: POL emigration shape is the most ambiguous of the seven; the EU-accession pull-factor dominates.*
- **BLR.** 200,000–500,000 Belarusians in exile post-August 2020 election (Parliamentary Assembly of Europe estimate, January 2023). ~2–5% of pre-crisis population of 9.5M. Includes government-in-exile under Tsikhanouskaya. https://jamestown.org/report/strategic-snapshot-five-years-since-belaruss-fraudulent-2020-presidential-election/ ; https://meduza.io/en/feature/2023/10/20/i-m-back-to-being-afraid
- **TUN.** 17,500+ Tunisians intercepted/disembarked in Italy in 2023 alone. Arab Barometer: "Tunisians emigrate in record numbers as hopes fade for its economy, democracy." Italian Florence court has refused deportations to Tunisia as "no longer safe" under Saied. https://www.arabbarometer.org/media-news/tunisians-emigrate-in-record-numbers-as-hopes-fade-for-its-economy-democracy/ ; https://www.euronews.com/2023/10/06/italian-court-blocks-deportation-of-migrant-to-tunisia-saying-its-not-a-safe-country
- **SLV.** Migration shape is *contested.* Bukele's claimed "migration reduction" largely mirrors Guatemala/Honduras (regional pattern), suggesting little Bukele-specific deterrent. **Internal displacement INCREASED**: 2023 report finds since 2022 state-of-exception, police and military have replaced gangs as the main drivers of IDPs. State violence (threats, misconduct, retaliation) is forcing intra-country relocation. https://cispes.org/article/decreasing-migration-el-salvador-another-bukele-myth ; https://scholars.org/contribution/el-salvadors-migration-success-story-doesnt ; https://internationalpolicy.org/publications/el-salvador-trump-and-the-transnational-state-of-exception/
- **BRA (recovery).** No comparable mass emigration shock under Bolsonaro; emigration-as-consequence literature is sparse for this case.

### Open questions

- Quantitative emigration estimates for SLV under the state-of-exception are poor; most existing work is either (a) regime PR, or (b) regional aggregates that hide a SLV-specific signal. Need finer-grained data.
- The "brain drain → recovery prospects" feedback loop (Auer/Schaub mechanism) has not been tested *symmetrically* on recovery cases. Does **reverse brain drain** (Poland-style) materially accelerate institutional restoration? Largely untested.
- IDP-vs-emigration substitution under hybrid civilian-authoritarian regimes (SLV being the marquee case) is under-theorized. The Bukele case may be the first major test where IDP, not cross-border emigration, is the dominant displacement channel.
- Belarus: precise demographic composition of the exile is poorly documented; sectoral/professional-skew estimates vary widely.

## 2. Economic consequences

### Key papers

- **CEPR DP20874 / CITP — "The Economic Consequences of Democratic Backsliding: Evidence from U.S. States" (2025).** — Cross-state panel: duration of backsliding episode negatively correlated with state GDP per capita; sharp increase in income inequality; business R&D and patenting contract — undermines "endogenous growth engine." https://citp.ac.uk/publications/the-economic-consequences-of-democratic-backsliding-evidence-from-us-states ; https://cepr.org/publications/dp20874
- **WZB Discussion Paper v25-502 — "The Economic Implications of Democratic Collapse."** https://bibliothek.wzb.eu/pdf/2025/v25-502.pdf
- **MDPI Economies (2025) — "Safeguarding Economic Growth Amid Democratic Backsliding."** Institutions outweigh innovation as a growth predictor under backsliding. https://www.mdpi.com/2227-7099/13/8/237
- **Acemoglu et al. (NBER/peer-reviewed) — 175 countries, 1960–2010: democratization raises GDP per capita by 20–25% over 25 years.** Referenced in Brookings synthesis. https://www.brookings.edu/articles/democracy-is-good-for-the-economy-can-business-defend-it/
- **Journal of Democracy (2025) — "Beyond Performance: Why Leaders Still Matter."** Counters the view that economic crisis automatically triggers backsliding; in most cases growth was stable for ≥5 years pre-onset. Implication: economic stress is *not* the universal trigger. https://www.journalofdemocracy.org/articles/beyond-performance-why-leaders-still-matter/

### Country-by-country findings

- **VEN.** Cleanest economic-collapse case in the modern record. GDP shrank from $196B (2013) → $80B (2018); cumulative ~73% contraction from 2014 to 2021 trough. Inflation: 69% (2014) → 274% (2016) → 4,000% (2017) → 130,060% (2018) → 9,586% (2019). Oil production: 3.0 mbpd (~2000) → 2.3 mbpd (pre-2014) → continued decline as PDVSA experts replaced with political appointees. https://www.economicsobservatory.com/why-did-venezuelas-economy-collapse ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyperinflation_in_Venezuela ; https://www.cfr.org/backgrounders/venezuela-crisis
- **TUR.** 2018 lira crisis (4.0→7.0 TRY/USD over five months) triggered by current account deficit + dollar-debt rollover risk. 2021 crisis: Erdoğan fired CB chief Ağbal, replaced with Kavcıoğlu, cut rates 19%→14% against IMF/orthodox advice; lira ₺8.5 (Sep 2021) → ₺26 (2023+). "Erdoğanomics": low rates as anti-inflation policy. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_economic_crisis_(2018%E2%80%93current) ; https://www.cnn.com/2021/03/22/economy/turkey-lira-erdogan-central-bank-intl-hnk ; http://www.cpreview.org/articles/2022/9/delusion-and-despotism-why-erdogans-monetary-policy-is-to-blame-for-turkeys-financial-crisis
- **HUN.** Stable stagnation thesis: GDP -0.9% in 2023; ~€18B EU funds frozen ≈10% of GDP. FDI/GDP fell from 76% (2009) → 60% (2022). Economy oriented around low-value-added auto assembly (Germany-dependent); productivity growth and R&D investment chronically below EU average. Orbán's loss of grip on power (April 2026 commentary) attributed to "Orbanomics failure." https://www.osw.waw.pl/en/publikacje/osw-commentary/2026-03-20/stable-stagnation-hungarys-economic-standing-after-16-years ; https://www.piie.com/blogs/realtime-economics/2026/what-orbans-ouster-hungary-means-europe ; https://www.irishtimes.com/world/europe/2026/04/13/orbanomics-failure-costs-hungarys-strongman-his-grip-on-power/
- **POL.** Strong growth 2014–2017 (>3% real); 2023 slowdown to 0.2% real growth. Deficit averaged 3.0% of GDP over decade to 2023. PiS implemented sectoral taxes (banks, insurance, energy, healthcare) targeting foreign-owned firms — dampened FDI sentiment but did not collapse the economy. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_Poland ; https://bti-project.org/fileadmin/api/content/en/downloads/reports/country_report_2024_POL.pdf — *Note: POL is the major outlier — backsliding without economic collapse.*
- **BLR.** GDP -4.7% in 2022 (post-Ukraine invasion sanctions); modest 0.9% recovery May 2023. Russia's share of total trade now ~70%; Russia controls ~90% of Belarus exports, ~80% of imports. ~65% of external debt is Russian-owned. Effectively a Russian economic protectorate post-2020. https://www.german-economic-team.com/en/newsletter/belarus-increasing-economic-dependence-on-russia/ ; https://www.osw.waw.pl/en/publikacje/osw-commentary/2023-08-18/towards-a-dependence-no-alternative-russias-increased-role
- **SLV.** Public debt 95.4% of GDP (2020). 18–23% of public budget servicing debt. $1.4B IMF bailout (2024) conditioned on Bitcoin pullback; Bukele continues 1-BTC/day purchases anyway. Reserves now ~15% in BTC ($513M as of Feb 2026, down from $658M end-2025). Bitcoin adoption never gained traction with population — pure sovereign exposure. https://www.cfr.org/articles/imf-bailing-out-el-salvador-it-shouldnt-be-so-lenient-cryptocurrency ; https://www.intellinews.com/bitcoin-selloff-exposes-cracks-in-el-salvador-s-sovereign-financing-strategy-426448/
- **TUN.** Decade of economic stagnation + IMF program rejection under Saied. Migration to Italy is partly economic-driven. Economy is the proximate driver behind Saied's anti-migrant scapegoating campaigns.

### Open questions

- Poland is the major outlier in the case set: severe institutional backsliding under PiS coexisted with continued strong growth and macro stability. This argues *against* a tight backsliding → economic decline causal mechanism in the EU institutional context (EU funds + single market act as a floor).
- Hungary EU-funds-conditionality literature is unusually mature; the Tandfonline 2025 paper on "selective compliance strategy" is directly portable to other EU-member backsliders. https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/13501763.2025.2553752
- The capital flight / FDI / inequality triangle is empirically thin specifically *for personalist civilian-authoritarian* cases (vs Cold-War juntas). Some cases (HUN, TUR) keep FDI nominally flowing while changing its composition (loyalist firms).

## 3. Recovery dynamics

### Theoretical literature

- **V-Dem WP 147 — "When Autocratization is Reversed: Episodes of Democratic Turnarounds since 1900."** Central typology: "U-Turn" episodes. **52% of all autocratization episodes become U-Turns; 73% in the last 30 years.** Average turnaround ~5 years after autocratization onset. **Active, coordinated, early resistance is the key predictor** — must engage within one electoral cycle (4–6 years). **Economic development predicts resilience to *onset* but not to *recovery*** — once autocratization begins, economic factors lose predictive power. https://v-dem.net/media/publications/wp_147_yvOYnKU.pdf
- **Tomini & Wunsch — Democratic Resilience as Two-Stage Process (Democratization 2021).** Distinguishes resistance to onset vs resistance to consolidation; "bounce back" resilience is the common path. https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/13510347.2021.1891413
- **"When autocratization is reversed: episodes of U-Turns since 1900" (Democratization 2024).** Updated U-Turn analysis. https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/13510347.2024.2448742
- **"Unity makes strength: Patterns of democratic resistance against autocratization" (EJPR).** Five recurrent reversal elements: (1) independent judiciary constraint on executive, (2) mass mobilization, (3) alternation in power, (4) opposition unification with civil society, (5) international democracy support. https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/european-journal-of-political-research/article/unity-makes-strength-patterns-of-democratic-resistance-against-autocratization/E7F241FD96CE2FAD8B1574C0A243D938
- **Riedl, McCoy, Roberts & Somer (2024) — "Pathways of Democratic Backsliding, Resistance, and (Partial) Recoveries." *Annals AAPSS*.** Major synthesis piece. https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/00027162251319909
- **Carnegie Endowment (2025) — Democratic Recovery After Significant Backsliding: Emergent Lessons.** Four case studies. **Of 25 countries that have undergone democratic backsliding episodes since 1990, only 4 have reversed the decline to original quality. Only Sri Lanka has sustained recovery >5 years; Czechia (2021), Guatemala and Poland (both 2023) are too recent to count as "sustained."** https://carnegieendowment.org/research/2025/04/democratic-recovery-after-significant-backsliding-emergent-lessons ; https://carnegieendowment.org/research/2025/03/lessons-about-backsliding-and-resistance
- **Bermeo, N. — "On Democratic Backsliding."** Origin of *executive aggrandizement* concept; three types (promissory coup, executive aggrandizement, strategic election manipulation). All seven of our cases plus IND are executive-aggrandizement type. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic_backsliding
- **Levitsky & Ziblatt — *How Democracies Die* (2018) + *Tyranny of the Minority* (2023).** Established framework: democracies die from within via legal-electoral means; gatekeeping failure is the proximate mechanism.
- **IDI / Toda Peace Institute / IFES policy briefs on Resilience and Repair (2025).** Practitioner-side roadmap literature. https://toda.org/policy-briefs-and-resources/policy-briefs/report-270-full-text.html ; https://www.ifes.org/pub/paths-democratic-resilience-era-backsliding/understanding-and-designing-resilience-interventions

### Recent recovery cases

#### POL 2023+ (Tusk reversal)
- Mandate: October 2023 election, broad democratic coalition wins after 8 years PiS.
- **Damage scale**: ~30% of judges are PiS-era appointees through captured National Council of the Judiciary. Constitutional Tribunal fully captured.
- **Reform tools**: Justice Minister Bodnar's "Action Plan" (Feb 2024); three acts on Constitutional Tribunal and National Council of the Judiciary passed.
- **Obstacles (active, not historical)**: PiS-aligned President Duda then Nawrocki (since Aug 2025) refer reform acts to PiS-captured Constitutional Tribunal which invalidates them. **Cohabitation + captured Tribunal = legislative paralysis.** Routine-legislative reform path is effectively blocked.
- **Verdict in literature**: "Trapped in institutional paralysis" (GMF); restoration "uncertain"; "warning from Poland." 18 months in, judicial reform stalled — but media, prosecutor's office, civic space have been partially restored.
- Sources: https://www.journalofdemocracy.org/articles/democracy-after-illiberalism-a-warning-from-poland/ ; https://www.gmfus.org/news/polands-rule-law-repair-trapped-institutional-paralysis ; https://verfassungsblog.de/restoring-the-rule-of-law/ ; https://eucrim.eu/news/poland-rule-of-law-developments-in-the-second-half-of-2024/ ; https://www.cer.eu/insights/difficulties-restoring-democracy-poland

#### BRA 2023+ (Lula reversal)
- Lula returns Jan 2023 after Jan 8 2023 attempted Capitol-style assault.
- **Brazil's institutions held**: TSE (electoral court), STF (supreme court) actively defended electoral system; military did not back Bolsonaro's challenge.
- **Recovery emphasis**: rebuilding Amazon protection, indigenous rights, environmental enforcement, scientific institutions (deliberately gutted under Bolsonaro). Foreign policy restoration (Unasur relaunch, climate summit hosting, COP30 in Belém 2025) used to re-anchor international credibility.
- **Persistent fragility**: extreme polarization; Bolsonaro still politically active (then jailed). "Lula's unfinished democracy" (Dissent).
- Sources: https://360info.org/lulas-mission-to-restore-brazilian-democracy/ ; https://revdem.ceu.edu/2025/05/23/brazils-democratic-resilience/ ; https://dissentmagazine.org/article/lulas-unfinished-democracy/ ; https://www.brookings.edu/articles/after-a-victory-for-democracy-what-is-brazils-road-ahead/

### Historical recovery cases

- **Sri Lanka 2015 (Sirisena) — REVERSED 2019.** Surprise defeat of Rajapaksa Jan 2015. 19th Constitutional Amendment (Apr 2015) reduced presidential powers, restored independent oversight commissions. **Then stalled** as Sirisena–Wickremesinghe coalition soured. October 2018 attempted "constitutional coup" (Sirisena tries to install Rajapaksa as PM, dissolve parliament — judiciary blocks). November 2019 Gotabaya Rajapaksa wins → 20th Amendment (2020) reverses the 19th. **Net: a 4-year recovery that fully reversed.** Carnegie now classifies SL as the only sustained recovery from its 1990+ dataset — *but* the V-Dem trajectory tells a more complicated story; need to clarify which window of SL we're calling "sustained." https://www.crisisgroup.org/asia/south-asia/sri-lanka/286-sri-lanka-s-transition-nowhere ; https://www.brookings.edu/articles/sri-lankas-presidential-elections-progress-regression-or-paralysis/
- **South Korea (1987 transition, consolidation through ~1998 first peaceful alternation).** Industrialization-first then democratization; vibrant civil society pre-existed transition. Now categorized as "fully consolidated liberal democracy" (third-wave success). https://accesson.kr/rks/assets/pdf/7596/journal-10-2-53.pdf
- **Chile (post-1990, post-Pinochet).** Concertación broad pro-democracy coalition; transitional justice via Rettig Commission; perpetrators prosecuted (notably late). Pinochet-era constitutional locks took decades to remove.
- **Indonesia (post-1998, post-Suharto).** Reformasi via mass mobilization + economic crisis. Encouraged women/youth participation. Now classified as partly free with backsliding concerns again under Prabowo.
- **Argentina (post-1983, deepened post-2003).** Trials of the Junta (1985), amnesty laws struck down as unconstitutional. Strong public norm against impunity. Currently testing under Milei — relevant ongoing case.
- General comparative finding: **Chile and Argentina built broad pro-democracy alliances**; **Chile and Indonesia encouraged women and youth participation**; **Chile pursued penalization + reparations; Argentina rejected immunity outright.** Mode of dealing with past regime varies and is itself a recovery-durability variable.

### What predicts recovery SPEED

Synthesizing across V-Dem U-Turn paper, Carnegie 2025, EJPR "Unity Makes Strength," and the recent recovery cases:

1. **Timing of resistance (largest single predictor).** Resistance within one electoral cycle (4–6 years) of autocratization onset → much higher U-Turn probability. Cases that wait beyond two cycles stall because institutional capture is fait accompli.
2. **Pre-existing institutional capacity that survives the autocratic period.** Brazilian electoral court (TSE) was strong enough that it could refuse Bolsonaro's challenge in real time. Poland inherits a *captured* Constitutional Tribunal — slower recovery.
3. **Opposition unification with civil society at the moment of electoral inflection.** Polish KO–TD–Lewica coalition (2023), Brazilian broad anti-Bolsonaro front (2022).
4. **Mode of electoral exit of the incumbent.** Defeat at the ballot (Tusk, Lula, Sirisena 2015) → faster reform onset than negotiated departures.
5. **International scaffolding (especially EU funds conditionality).** Hungary case shows the *constraint side* of this lever; Poland 2023+ shows the *unlocking side* — €137B in suspended funds was conditional on rule-of-law restoration, giving Tusk leverage.

### What predicts recovery DURABILITY

Where the literature is *thinnest* (Carnegie 2025 explicitly: "literature on successful re-democratisation is thin"). Hypothesized predictors with empirical support:

1. **Party-system structure vs single-leader autocratization.** Personalist civilian-authoritarian regimes (Bukele, Erdoğan) may be more reversible *but* leave less institutional residue; party-state fusions (PiS, Fidesz) embed more deeply and slow durable recovery. **This is the central open variable for our 7-case set.**
2. **Survival of independent judiciary remnants.** Brazil yes, Poland no (Constitutional Tribunal captured), Sri Lanka partially. The Polish recovery is being delayed *precisely because* the captured Tribunal blocks reforms — a perverse case of an autocratized institution preventing its own un-autocratization.
3. **Polarization legacy.** Affective polarization persists *after* democratic restoration begins; citizens don't "walk back" support for norm-violations even when their side returns to power. Brazilian and US literatures both find this. (Science Advances; Riedl et al.)
4. **Reversal of executive-aggrandizement institutional changes (Bermeo's framework).** Sri Lanka 2015 successfully reversed via 19th Amendment — and reversed *back* via 20th Amendment in 2020. Constitutional supermajority threshold is itself a durability variable.
5. **Treatment of the old regime.** Argentina's Junta trials + Chile's Rettig Commission embedded a non-impunity norm that has held. Sri Lanka attempted no transitional justice for the war or for Rajapaksa-era abuses — directly correlated with the 2019 reversal.
6. **Media reform.** Captured media oligarchy and state-broadcaster politicization persist past the restoring election; recovery durability appears low when media remains aligned with the old regime. Hungary now testing this in reverse direction post-2026.

### Accelerators to recovery

- **EU conditionality** (Poland 2023+ unlock; Hungary 2022+ pressure).
- **Civil society organization that survived the autocratization period** — student movements, lawyers' associations, independent judges' bar. Strongest evidence: Brazil 2022, Poland 2023.
- **Judicial remnants** strong enough to refuse autocratic orders in real time (Brazil TSE/STF; Sri Lanka 2018 Supreme Court blocking Sirisena's coup attempt).
- **UN/international democracy support** — North Macedonia (Gruevski reversal) is the cleanest case of international-pressure-as-accelerator (EJPR paper).
- **Reverse brain drain** — under-tested but Poland's tech-sector reverse migration from 2023 suggests a possible accelerator channel; theoretically symmetric to Auer/Schaub's brain-drain mechanism.

### Holds-up / obstacles to recovery

- **Captured Constitutional Court / apex judiciary** (Poland Constitutional Tribunal; Hungary Constitutional Court; Venezuela TSJ). These bodies can invalidate restoration legislation while themselves immune from reform via routine legislation.
- **Captured media oligarchy** (Hungary KESMA, Venezuela state media, El Salvador's social-media-first ecosystem under Bukele).
- **Electoral law entrenchment** — gerrymandering, voter registration rules, election commission composition lock in advantage past the transition.
- **Party-state institutional fusion** vs **single-leader cult.** Fidesz embedded deeper than personalist Bukele; predicts slower Hungarian recovery if/when it begins.
- **Cohabitation with old-regime presidency** (Poland: Duda → Nawrocki). Veto power survives the parliamentary turnover. Single most consequential institutional drag on Polish restoration speed.
- **Polarization persistence** (Science Advances 2024) — voters do not de-escalate after restoration.
- **Continued political viability of former autocrat** (Bolsonaro until conviction; Rajapaksa returning via brother; Orbán post-2026; potentially Bukele after presidency).

## Suggested integration with our pattern work

For the Bukelization no-coup pattern (pattern 013):

- The seven cases plus India fall under Bermeo's **executive aggrandizement** type — separates them cleanly from coup-mediated cases and grounds the "no-coup" criterion theoretically.
- Migration consequences are *strongly attested* for VEN/TUR/HUN/BLR/TUN; *contested* for POL (EU pull-factor confound) and SLV (IDP dominant); *absent* for BRA (recovery case did not endure a long enough autocratization).
- Economic consequences are *strongly attested* for VEN/TUR; *moderate* for HUN/BLR; *muted* for POL/SLV/TUN. Poland is the major counter-case for any general "backsliding causes economic collapse" claim — likely because EU funds + single market act as a floor.
- Recovery durability is the **least well-understood** variable in the literature. Our case set (POL/BRA recovery + Sri Lanka 2015 historical) gives us the right comparators. **The party-state-fusion-vs-personalist axis is the most promising untested predictor** for durability — and our seven cases stratify cleanly on it (HUN/POL = party-state; SLV/TUR/VEN = personalist; BLR = hybrid; TUN = personalist-recent).
