## Meta-patterns emerging from Tier C dig (2026-05-25)

The Tier C dig surfaced a **conflict-type meta-pattern** that may govern the whole corpus:
1. **Formal-army war** (ETH Federal-TPLF, UKR Russo-Ukrainian): very high fatality, low displacement-per-fatality (~30-80), spatial concentration, no strife channel
2. **Predator-militia campaigns vs civilians** (DRC ISCAP+M23, BFA government counter-ops with civilian collateral): mid fatality, very high displacement-per-fatality (~250-800), spatial concentration, mixed strife+one-sided channels
3. **Irregular insurgency** (Sahel JNIM/ISGS, Somalia Al-Shabaab): low-to-mid fatality, mid displacement-per-fatality, spatial diffusion, strife-dominant
4. **Bimodal mega-floods** (PAK 2010/2022, NER 2024): displacement spikes can dominate decades; not steady-trend

If this meta-typology firms up after Tier D + South Asia digs, it could be the **main argument of the methodology paper**: corpus heterogeneity isn't noise, it's a small number of distinguishable conflict-types each needing different displacement models.
