# Pattern 028 — Thailand as second Regime 1 member (PAK no longer structurally unique)

## One line

THA (Thailand) classifies as Regime 1 (Bimodal-mega-flood) under PRE_REG_003 strict rules: flood 94.3%, storm 5.7%, ≥2 mega-flood years. **PAK is no longer uniquely Regime 1.**

Dominant flood event: 2011 Thai floods (Chao Phraya basin, ~813K displaced; total event ~12.8M affected).

## Why it stands out

PRE_REG_003 originally treated PAK as structurally unique due to Indus geography (steep Himalayan gradient + glacial melt + monsoon belt + flat Indus plain). THA's match suggests the **bimodal-mega-flood mechanism generalizes to similar geographies**: monsoon belt + major lowland river basin (Chao Phraya analogous to Indus). 

**Tentative claim**: Regime 1 may have 2-4 members globally (PAK Indus, THA Chao Phraya, possibly Mekong basin states, possibly BGD pre-cyclone-era).
