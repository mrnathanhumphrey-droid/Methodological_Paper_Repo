## Why it stands out
Earthquakes are discrete sudden-onset events (unlike drought) that don't track governance (unlike conflict) and aren't seasonal (unlike flood). When they hit a vulnerable population they displace at scale immediately. The SYR Feb 2023 EQ struck on top of an existing civil war population — compound crisis. HTI's 2010 earthquake (>1.5M displaced, not in this acute panel — pre-crisis row) plus the 2021 Saint-Domingue EQ (the acute 224K) make earthquake a recurring HTI driver. Not a channel for analysis everywhere, but for SYR / HTI / SLV / Andean LatAm it's a separately-identifiable cell.

## Open questions
- Does adding earthquake as a fourth channel improve cell-residue fit, or does it look like a one-time discrete shock that doesn't model well?
- Are earthquake-displaced populations the same as conflict-displaced (compounding) or geographically separate?
- Should the channel taxonomy be hazard-specific (flood / drought / EQ / storm) or should "natural disaster" stay as one channel with hazard-type as covariate?
